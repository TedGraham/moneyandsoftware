    The Great Pomona Book List

    Ted Graham October 22, 2006

    “I have said that in my whole life, I’ve known no wise person over a broad subject matter area who didn’t read all the time–none, zero.” — Charlie Munger (partner in Berkshire Hathaway)

    My sophomore year in college was 1994, and email was just starting to catch on.  I worked in the campus lab helping people print and explaining that I couldn’t help when they typed more than 256 characters into the email editor without hitting return, which overflowed the buffer, thereby losing their email.

    As a budding programmer, I was thrilled to discover a hack that would allow me to send email to everyone on campus.  I figured it would only work once, so we debated what to send for a couple of days.  My roommates Steve and Mira helped me come up with the question: “What are your five favorite books?”

    Pomona only had about 1400 students, so the email system survived.  We got about 500 responses and I was told that my job depended on not doing that again.  We manually compiled everyone’s reccomendations into a giant list, and sent that back out to all respondees.  Twelve years later, that list is finally on the web, ordered by the number of votes received.
